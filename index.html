State, not a smart contract

Three inscriptions.
One balance model.

The original BRC-20 documentation describes an off-chain balance state aggregated from deploy, mint, and transfer activity. The ownership of the first mint recipient and first transfer recipient is part of the state transition.

01 / DEPLOY

Defines the policy

Deploy creates a case-insensitive ticker record and defines maximum supply, optional mint limit, and optional precision. It does not itself add balance.

02 / MINT

Credits first owner

A valid mint credits only the first owner of its inscription. This is why an intermediary inscription service can accidentally receive the balance.

03 / TRANSFER

Locks, then settles

A transfer inscription deducts available balance and becomes a transferable. Its first on-chain movement to another owner settles the transfer.

Transfer state machine

Do not stop at the inscription.

The most important BRC-20 UX distinction is between a valid transferable and a settled recipient balance. Display these states separately in wallet and product flows.

01

Read available balance

Available equals overall balance minus amounts already locked in active transfer inscriptions.

02

Inscribe transfer

Create a valid transfer with an amount no larger than the available balance. The amount becomes transferable.

03

Protect the satpoint

Keep the transferable inscription out of automatic coin selection and fund fees with separate cardinal sats.

04

Send to settle

Move the exact inscription to the recipient. The first transfer of that inscription credits their balance.

Original operational warnings

Rules that change outcomes.

These are the conditions that make a seemingly normal Bitcoin transaction produce an unexpected BRC-20 result.

  • The first valid deployment claims a ticker, and ticker comparison is case-insensitive.
  • Block confirmation order resolves same-block conflicts, including multiple transfer inscriptions.
  • A transfer inscription only locks the sender amount. It does not credit the recipient until the inscription is sent.
  • Sending a valid transfer inscription back to its current owner restores the amount to available balance.
  • Do not use a non-Ordinals-compatible wallet destination for an inscription transfer.
  • Never let an inscription service receive a mint or transfer first unless that service is intended to own the protocol result.
